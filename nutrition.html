POWERLIFTING
NUTRITION

Main focus: caloric intake

Powerlifters are among the athletes with the highest required caloric intake, due to the high training intensity and to recover fast enough for the next training session or competition. Even in the off-season you are advised to stay in a caloric surplus. The International Society of Sports Nutrition suggests 40-70 kcals/kg/day for strength athletes training 2-3 hours daily, compared to 25-35 kcals/kg/day for the average fitness enthusiast.

Macronutrient balance

Which nutrients do we need to focus on? Aside from a healthy balance in vitamins and minerals, which everyone needs, proteins are the top priority, as they are the primary building blocks for your muscles. The suggestion here is 1.6-1.7g protein/kg/day in 20g servings, 5-6 times daily. Carbohydrates provide energy and refill glycogen storages. This is particularly helpful before (and after) training sessions. The suggested intake is 4-7g/kg/day.

Supplements

There is an astronomic amount of supplements available on the market. However, 95% of these have been unsufficiently tested to prove their alledged effects. At this moment these are the most researched and scientifically backed supplements:

Whey protein
Whey is a concentrated protein supplement in powder form. During the production of cheese, whey is produced as a byproduct. You can mix this powder with water, milk, yoghurt etc, to make your protein intake cheaper and less time-consuming.
Caffein
Just like jumpstarting your day with a morning coffee, caffein can be consumed in other forms, in powder or as a pill, to enhance your energy production during training.
Creatine
Last but definitely not least. Creatine is a powerfull tool in your arsenal if you want to take your training to the next level. This supplement improves hydration and stored energy levels in muscle cells. If you want to know more, check out this article on Healthline: Healthline - Creatine
