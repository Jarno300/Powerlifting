Training basics

Training is, next to nutrition, the most important part of your journey. Like nutrition there is a lot of misinformation out there. Mainly content creators will claim certain exercises or training plans are revolutionary, just to keep the content going. This way the pool of information around this subject becomes heavily dilluted. On this page we summarize the most researched and most commonly recommended training programs available.

The absolute beginner

Never held a barbell in your life? Start here. This program is designed to get used to the main movements. Once you feel comfortable and your technique is solid, move on to one of the more refined routines.

r/Fitness Basic Beginner Routine

GZCL

GZCLP is a beginner to intermediate level linear progression program, consisting of three lifts each workout day. It offers a significantly more balanced exercise selection and effective progression than most other linear progression programs, especially with regards to squat vs deadlift balance.

r/Fitness GZCL

5-3-1

A popular, proven system for training that is effective for trainees of all levels by veteran coach Jim Wendler, which has many common variants available for free online.

r/Fitness 5/3/1 for Beginners

nSuns LP

A “linear progression” focused program based very loosely on elements of 5/3/1 Spinal Tap, created by r/Fitness regular nSuns. Comes in 4 day, 5 day, and 6 day variations.

r/Fitness nSuns LP

Conclusion

Consistancy is key, no matter what program you decide to follow. On the other hand it's not a bad idea to change things up every now an then. You can find an extended archive of routines at The Fitness Wiki
